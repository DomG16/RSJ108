How to make Your Advertisement Stand Out In Journalism

Assembly time: 30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Advertising is the best way to bring attention to your company or cause. Knowing how to create that attention and making a powerful advertisement is something you have to learn but is easy to do so. It can be a lot of fun too!

  • What You'll Need

  • Research
  • Imagination
  • Design Skills
  • Branding
  • A Strong Message

  1. Research your audience.

    If you want to attract a specific audience, you have to know who they are first. Do research on those who are already customers of your brand and why they chose it. Findings things such as age, geographic location and interests will help you know who to target.

  2. Be Personal.

    If an ad feels personal, customers are more likely to grow a connection to your brand. Using words like “your,” or “our,” will make the customer feel like they’re having a face-to-face conversation with you and your brand.

    Conversation bubbles.

  3. Make your headline attractive.

    The first thing someone is going to see on your ad is the headline. If it’s bland and boring, they won’t be interested in the rest of the ad. Make it big, and propose some kind of question or bold statement that leaves the customer on a cliffhanger so they’ll want to see more.

  4. Be short and simple with your message.

    No one wants to see an ad that looks like an essay. If your reader has to read a lot, chances are they aren’t going to be interested. Whatever your message is, make it short, but make sure it’s clear what the message is. That way customers will have an understanding and will want to learn more.

  5. Have a clear call to action.

    Being short and simple ties into being clear. Are you advertising a new product? Are you offering a coupon or sale? Have a clear call to action and how customers can benefit from that action.

  6. Visuals are everything.

    Some ads don’t include images, but the vast majority do. Before we read something, we’ll see something. Including unique images or artwork to attract the readers eye will make them more interested in the rest of the ad, especially if they share interest with the image.

  7. Make your brand clear.

    No matter how nice an ad can look, customers won’t be interested if they don’t know what your brand is. Using images, logos, or slogans will make your brand easily recognizable and more attractive.

  8. Optimize your ads for the web, not just print.

    Before technology and social media, it was easy to just print an ad and hang it up on a building. Now, people are constantly on their phones and laptops, seeing ads all over. If your ad doesn’t fit their screen or is hard to read, they’ll either miss key information or will just scroll away. Making sure your ad will fit on a phone screen, laptop screen etc. will increase engagement on your ad.
